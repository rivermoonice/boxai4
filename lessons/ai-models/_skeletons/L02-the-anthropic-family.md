## Suggested Pitfalls (for §4)

1. **Paying for Fable 5 expecting Mythos-class intelligence at a daily-driver price.** Per the channel's belief, Fable 5's "real-world cost makes it unusable as a daily driver until pricing improves."
2. **Assuming all Claude models are equally good at coding.** The index shows Opus 4.7 was a regression; Sonnet is more reliable; Fable 5 is the new top but expensive.
3. **Locking into the Max plan before testing.** The channel flags "they don't actually tell you how much you'll actually have within your plan."
4. **Ignoring the cost-tier trade-off.** Per the channel, "Practical, cost-efficient models like Kimi K2.7 and MiniMax M3 beat raw frontier capability for everyday use."
5. **Confusing Opus 4.6, 4.7, and 4.8.** Three different releases, three different reliability profiles. Read the verdict for the specific version, not "Claude Opus."

## Glossary Seeds

- **Opus** — Anthropic's flagship model (currently 4.8). Most expensive, most capable.
- **Sonnet** — Anthropic's mid-tier model. Cheaper than Opus, faster, less reliable on hard problems.
- **Fable 5** — Anthropic's newest model (June 2026). Mythos-class intelligence, expensive, not yet a daily driver.
- **Mythos-class** — intelligence tier above standard frontier. The channel's framing for Fable 5.
- **Max plan** — Claude Code's $200/month subscription. Source video: "they don't actually tell you how much you'll actually have."
- **One-shot** — solving a problem in a single attempt, without iteration.
- **Quantized leftover** — per the channel, Anthropic's "quietly reduced" compute allocation for cheaper tiers may serve consumer users "the quantized leftovers" of Mythos compute.
